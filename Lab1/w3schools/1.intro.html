How did whales appear?

The first whales appeared 50 million years ago, well after the extinction of the dinosaurs, but
well before the appearance of the first humans. Their ancestor is most likely an ancient
artiodactyl, i.e. a four-legged, even-toed hoofed (ungulate) land mammal, adapted for running. Cetaceans thus have a common ancestor with modern-day artiodactyls such as the cow, the pig,
the camel, the giraffe and the hippopotamus.

Why haven't whales fully evolved?

Whales and dolphins did not evolve gills because they are mammals. This means that the time
that elapsed between their earliest ancestors leaving the water to become land animals is much greater than the time elapsed since their ancestors returned to the ocean.
